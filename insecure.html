Welcome to the insecure version of my web page. Let me explain what you're seeing here

All of my normal content is served over SSL. I do this because I believe strongly in maintaining at least some sort of privacy while browsing. Therefore, I have set up my own Root CA Certificate and used that to secure everything on my server.

There's one problem, though. Your browser most likely doesn't recognize me as a Certification Authority, and it shouldn't by default. This means that if you proceed to my secure site, you'll get a nasty-looking warning about security and the site not being verified by a trusted CA. This is normal.

At that point, you have 3 options:

  • Proceed to the site, and the warning. You'll be fine, but I wouldn't recommend doing this anywhere else.
  • Don't proceed to the site and miss out on all my awesome secure content. You'll probably still be fine.
  • Install my root certificate in your trusted root CA store so you never have to see that warning again. If you know what this means, this is probably the option for you - especially if you know me and trust me.
